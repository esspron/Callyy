Is h200 overkill
Using an NVIDIA H200 GPU for your Voicory voice AI stack can be considered an overkill in the early-to-mid stage of scaling due to the following reasons:

Cost Efficiency:
H200 GPUs are powerful but costly—rental or purchase costs are significantly higher than A100 or similar GPUs typically used for large-scale voice and language model inference. For a startup or growing SaaS business, the H200’s high cost can dramatically increase operational expenses without proportionate gains in throughput early on.

Current Latency and Scale Needs:
Your expected workload in the first 6–12 months (up to 1 million minutes monthly) can be efficiently handled by a cluster of A100 GPUs or similar hardware, which are industry-proven for voice AI, real-time inference, and cost-effective[RunPod A100 ~₹50–100/hr]. H200’s extra performance is more justifiable only at very high scale (multi-million minutes with ultra-low latency SLAs) or heavy multi-task AI workloads beyond your current scope.

Software Ecosystem Maturity:
The A100 has mature support across Kubernetes autoscalers, Ray Serve, and model optimizers (e.g., Faster Whisper, Ray Serve), whereas H200 adoption is still ramping up. This means better stability and reliability with A100 clusters for your current application.

Optimal Scaling Strategy:
Start with A100 clusters for warm GPU pools and autoscaling to balance cost and performance. You can upgrade to H200 when you hit tens of millions of minutes monthly or require ultra-low latency <50ms at global scale. This staged upgrade reduces risk and maximizes ROI.

Summary

Factor	Recommendation	Comment
Early to Mid Scale	Use A100 GPU clusters	Cost-effective, proven, sufficient scale
High Volume + Low Latency	Consider H200 after scale-up	Justify with 10M+ min/month & <50ms SLA
Software Ecosystem	A100 is mature, stable	Faster dev, less risk
Budget	Optimize cost vs performance	A100 ≈ ₹50/hr vs H200 ≈ 2-3× cost
Bottom line: For the next 6–12 months of scaling your voice AI with mixed model calls, an NVIDIA H200 is overkill. Stick with A100 GPU clusters for maximal cost-efficiency and scalability. Upgrade to H200 when your volume or latency requirements outgrow A100 capabilities.
